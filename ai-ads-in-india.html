AI ads in India: the complete guide.

Everything a founder or marketing lead needs to understand AI advertising in India: what it is, what it costs, how it stacks up against a traditional agency, how fast it moves, and how to pick a studio that actually delivers results. This is the hub; the deeper dives are linked throughout.

Parth AgarwalRetency Studio12 min read

In two years, AI has gone from a novelty in Indian advertising to the way a growing share of performance creative actually gets made. Brands like SuperMoney, SHEIN, and XTEP are running it at scale. This guide explains how it works, what it costs, and how to use it without ending up with generic output that no one watches.

What are AI ads?

AI ads are advertising creatives, usually short video, produced with generative AI tools directed by human creative strategy, instead of a traditional film shoot. The distinction that matters is not "made by a machine." It is that the cost of producing the next variant of an ad drops close to zero.

A conventional shoot carries fixed costs, crew, talent, location, equipment, that you pay whether you make one ad or reshoot it five times. AI-native production strips most of that out. That single change is what makes AI ads powerful: testing ten concepts, the thing that actually finds you a winner, goes from impossible to routine.

Why are Indian brands switching to AI ads?

Three reasons, in order of how much they matter.

Speed. A traditional agency's production timeline runs in weeks. An AI-native studio ships a performance creative in about five business days, and can compress that to under 24 hours when a sale day demands it. For a brand that needs to test ten hooks before Diwali, that difference is decisive.

Cost of iteration. Performance marketing is a search for the winning creative. The channel that lets you search cheaply wins. Because each variant is inexpensive, you can afford to test angles, hooks, and formats the way the auction actually rewards.

Ownership of outcomes. The best AI studios are run by performance marketers, not just artists, so they are judged on whether the ad converts, not on whether an asset was delivered. For SuperMoney, roughly 70% of the creative in their public Meta Ad Library is ours, because the work performs and the engagement keeps growing.

How much do AI ads cost in India?

At Retency, AI ad creatives run from about ₹5,000 to ₹15,000 per video depending on craft level, with monthly retainers lowering the effective per-asset cost. A sensible first move is a test batch of 4 to 6 creatives, run with a modest media spend, so the data picks your winner before you scale.

The number that actually matters is not the sticker price; it is cost-per-winning-ad, and that is decided by research and iteration rather than by the tools. A ₹2,000 creative that never converts is more expensive than a ₹10,000 one that funds itself on day one. The full breakdown, per-video bands and retainer math, is in our AI ads pricing guide, and current rates are on the pricing page.

Rule of thumb: if you plan to test more than 6 to 8 creatives a month, a retainer beats per-video pricing on cost-per-winning-ad. For a one-off launch burst, per-video is cleaner.

Are AI ads better than a traditional agency?

It depends on your stage. For a growth-stage or D2C brand where performance is the goal and budget efficiency matters, an AI-native studio wins on speed, cost, and iteration. For a large brand running a single flagship film where production values are the entire message, a traditional shoot can still justify its cost. Increasingly, brands use both: the agency for the tentpole film, an AI studio for the always-on performance creative that drives the funnel.

The stereotype that AI creative looks cheap is three years out of date. In 2026 the quality depends entirely on the studio, because the tools are commoditised. What separates good work from slop is exactly what separated good agencies from bad ones: research, direction, and finishing. We go deeper in AI ads vs a traditional agency.

How fast can AI ads be produced?

Standard delivery at Retency is about five business days per video, informed by category research before a single frame is produced. When the moment demands it, the pipeline compresses further. During a SHEIN Slay Sale we delivered sale-specific creative on a same-day, sub-24-hour turnaround. The speed is not a stunt; it is what lets creative move at the speed of the market instead of the speed of a shoot. The exact hour-by-hour workflow is in our 24-hour ad playbook.

What makes an AI ad actually perform?

Four things, and none of them are the software:

  • Research depth. Studying the winning hooks, formats, and angles in your category before producing anything. This is the single biggest driver of whether an ad converts.
  • Iteration. One ad is a gamble. Performance comes from shipping variants: different hooks, different first three seconds, different calls to action.
  • Consistency. Keeping a recurring character, product, or identity coherent across many assets. It is genuinely hard with AI, and it is where craft separates studios.
  • Finishing. Sound design, pacing, captioning, and per-placement versions. The last 10% is what makes an ad look like a brand made it, not a prompt.

What can you make with AI beyond ads?

Performance creative is the entry point, but the same pipeline builds far more: brand films, recurring mascots, and full episodic worlds. It is our research lab: the hardest problems in AI creative, character consistency and long-form coherence, show up in an anime series first, and the solutions flow straight back into client work. More on that in how we are building Swarajya.

How do I choose an AI creative studio in India?

Judge the portfolio, not the label. Ask for real client outcomes with specific numbers. Confirm the studio does genuine category research rather than generating blind. Check that it iterates on winners instead of polishing losers, and that it owns the result rather than handing over an asset and walking away. And make sure the finishing is there, because that is where cheap fast work falls apart.

The short version: AI ads let Indian brands treat creative as a testable, repeatable growth channel instead of a one-off gamble. Pay for the thinking, insist on real proof, and the creative pays for itself.
