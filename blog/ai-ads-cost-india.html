Pricing

How much do AI ads cost in India? A 2026 pricing guide.

Straight answer: good AI ad creatives in India run from about ₹5,000 to ₹15,000 per video, with retainers changing the math entirely. Here's what sits behind those numbers, and how to tell whether you're paying for output or for outcomes.

Parth Agarwal21 Jul 20268 min read

Every founder who's thought about running paid ads eventually hits the same wall: the quote from a traditional production house makes the whole channel look unaffordable. A single polished video ad can cost ₹50,000 to several lakh, take three weeks, and give you exactly one asset to test. If it flops, and most first attempts do, you've burned the budget before you learned anything.

AI-native creative changes that equation. Not because it's "cheaper AI slop," but because it collapses the cost of iteration. When producing the fifth variant costs a fraction of the first, you can afford to test angles, hooks, and formats the way performance marketing actually demands. Below is an honest breakdown of what AI ads cost in India in 2026, and what you're really paying for.

The short answer: per-video and retainer pricing

There are two ways studios price AI ad creative, and they suit different stages of business:

1. Per-video (project) pricing

Best when you're testing the waters or need a specific burst of assets. In India, expect roughly:

  • ₹5,000 – ₹8,000 per video, UGC-style and static-motion ads, script-to-screen, built for Meta and Reels. This is the volume-testing tier.
  • ₹8,000 – ₹15,000 per video, higher-craft brand films, character-led concepts, and product hero pieces that need more direction and finishing.
  • ₹15,000+ per video, cinematic work, original-IP style films, and anything requiring bespoke character consistency across scenes.

At Retency, our AI performance creatives sit in the first two bands, and each is delivered in about five business days, informed by category research before a single frame is produced, not generated blind.

2. Retainer pricing

Best when ads are a channel, not an experiment. A monthly retainer buys a volume of creative plus the strategy layer around it, testing plans, iteration on winners, and (if you want) media management. Retainers lower your effective per-asset cost significantly because the research and brand-learning is amortised across everything you ship.

Rule of thumb: if you plan to test more than 6–8 creatives a month, a retainer almost always beats per-video pricing on cost-per-winning-ad. If you need a one-off launch burst, project pricing is cleaner.

What actually drives the price

Two "AI ads" can differ 5× in price for reasons that have nothing to do with the AI. Here's where the real cost lives:

Research depth. A ₹5,000 ad and a ₹500 ad can use the same tools. The difference is whether someone studied the winning hooks, formats and angles in your category first. Cheap creative skips this, which is why it doesn't convert. Research is the single biggest driver of whether an ad performs.

Iteration count. One ad is a gamble. Performance comes from shipping variants, different hooks on the same concept, different first-three-seconds, different CTAs. Pricing that includes iteration is more expensive per engagement but far cheaper per result.

Character & brand consistency. Keeping a recurring character, product, or visual identity coherent across many assets is genuinely hard with AI, and it's where craft separates studios. It costs more because it's worth more, consistency compounds brand recall.

Finishing. Sound design, pacing, captioning, aspect-ratio versions for each placement. The "last 10%" is what makes an ad look like a brand made it, not a prompt.

The cheapest ad is the one that works. A ₹2,000 creative that never converts is infinitely more expensive than a ₹10,000 one that funds itself on day one.

Why AI ads cost less than a traditional shoot

A conventional video ad carries fixed costs that don't scale down: crew, location, talent, equipment, edit suites. You pay them whether you make one ad or ten, and you pay them again for every reshoot. AI-native production removes most of that fixed cost, so the marginal cost of the next variant approaches zero.

That's the real unlock. It's not that a single AI ad is dramatically cheaper than a single shot ad (though it usually is). It's that testing ten concepts, the thing that actually finds you a winner, goes from impossible to routine. We've built entire ad systems for brands like Shivora Ghee and Parv Essentials on exactly this principle: launch, read the data, double down on what works, kill what doesn't.

What you should not pay for

  • "Unlimited revisions" with no strategy. Endless tweaks to a bad concept is churn, not iteration. You want a studio testing new angles, not polishing a loser.
  • Per-hour billing on generative work. You're buying outcomes and assets, not someone's afternoon. Fixed per-asset or retainer pricing aligns incentives.
  • Volume with no research. A hundred cheap ads that all miss the hook are worth less than five that were built on real category insight.

A simple way to budget

If you're starting out, here's a sane first move: budget for a test batch of 4–6 creatives (roughly ₹25,000–₹60,000 depending on craft level), run them with a modest media spend, and let the data tell you which direction to scale. That's enough to learn what your audience responds to without betting the quarter on one guess.

Once you have a winner, shift to a retainer so you can iterate on it continuously. That's the path from "we tried ads once" to "ads are a reliable growth channel."

The honest summary: AI ads in India cost ₹5,000–₹15,000 per video, but the number that matters is cost-per-winning-ad, and that's decided by research and iteration, not by tools. Pay for the thinking, and the creative pays for itself.
